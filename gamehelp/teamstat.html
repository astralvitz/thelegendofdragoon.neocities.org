Stat and Team Evaluation

Ok, before I get started, I will make one thing clear: YOU choose the party that YOU like the best. I've written this here as a "personal evaluation". This does not mean that you should choose the party that I thought was the best, or anything like that. Use this as only an "outline", not as a strict guide. Choose the party YOU feel most comfortable with.

With that said, let me explain what this is all about. When you have all seven characters (Dart, Rose, Haschel, Albert, Meru, Kongol, Miranda) there are a total of 15 different parties you can choose from. What I have done is I've rated all 15 parties based on their "Specific Powers" and HP.

When you go into the Menu Screen, there is a sub-menu called "Status". Here in the status menu, you get to see different categories of all the characters, whether active or not. There are two status menus:

The first status menu is the "Overall Power" menu. The categories are:

Fighter Name and Level
D'LV: Dragoon Level and current SP
HP: Current/Maximum Hit Points
MP: Current/Maximum Magic Points
EXP: Current Experience Points/Experience Points needed to gain a level

The second status menu is "Specific Power" menu. The categories are:

AT: Physical Attack Power
DF: Physical Defense Power
MAT: Magical/Item Attack Power (determines how strong an Attacking Item is when used by a character)
MDF: Magical/Item Defense Power
Speed: Speed (determines attacking order; the higher the speed, the more likely to attack first)
A-Hit: Attack success percentage
M-Hit: Magic/Item success percentage
A-AV: Attack evasion percentage
M-AV: Magic/Item evasion percentage

Body, Weapon, Total, and Dragoon: This calculates how powerful each of the above categories is. First is "Body", which is simply how strong your character's body is. Next is "Weapon". This is the power of the weapon, armor, and accessory equipped to that character. "Total" is the sum of Body + Weapon. "Dragoon" refers to how much the category is increased when in Dragoon mode (this only applies to AT, DF, MAT, and MDF, and only when the character has the ability to turn into a Dragoon). For example, if "170%" is next to the AT under Dragoon, then the AT of that individual is increased 170%, or 1.7 times. This means that if your regular total AT is 150, then in Dragoon Mode the AT will be 255. (1.7 x 150 = 105, 105 + 150 = 255).

The other two menus in the Status sub-menu are the Equipped Menu (shows what weapon, armor, helmet, foot armor, and accessory are equipped) and the Magic Menu (shows what magic that character can cast in Dragoon Mode and how much MP is consumed after casting it).

All right then, let me explain how I calculated the team evaluation. First off, I did this while on the Moon. Dart was at level 54 and everyone else was from 46-50. I equipped to Kongol his first weapon, armor, helmet, foot armor, and accessory. Then I looked at what was available to everyone else at roughly the same time (in the Home of the Gigantos, where Kongol joins your party) and equipped him or her appropriately. Everyone had a bracelet on. The reason I choose Kongol is because he is the "last" team member you will get (when you recruit Miranda, Shana's stats become Miranda's stats, so I didn't count her).

After this was done, I wrote down the 15 different parties available and rated the HP and everything in the "Specific Powers" menu on a scale of 1-5.

1: Poor, the worst of the worst
2: Ok, could perhaps be better though...
3: Average, normal
4: Pretty good
5: Excellent, the best of the best

Here are the 15 parties available and their evaluation. Remember, Dart is always in the party. Also, this is ONLY AN EVALUATION OF MY OPINION! Choose the party YOU want, not what I say.

This is my favorite team, mainly because of the brute strength, high HP, and incredible defense they possess (combined, this is the highest out of all the teams). Keep in mind, though, that this is the slowest team available, and magic-wise they aren't very powerful overall (combined, they have the lowest MAT and MDF out of all the parties). Their A-AV and M-AV is 0% all the way through, so they're more likely to get hit than any of the other teams. However, these faults can always be cured with equipped the right accessories (like the Bandit's Ring, which increases speed). Overall, this is a very good team when you need strength to win.
